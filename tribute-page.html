Angel Locsin is a Filipina television and film actress, commercial model, film producer and fashion designer. On GMA Network from 2002, she came to prominence for her television roles as Alwina in the 2004 fantasy-themed television series Mulawin and as the superheroine Darna in the TV adaptation of the Mars Ravelo eponymous comic. Locsin was a contract artist of GMA Network up to early 2007 until she transferred to its rival network, ABS-CBN. She then starred as Lyka in the television series Lobo, which earned her an International Emmy Award nomination for best performance. She later starred in the films In the Name of Love and One More Try, for which she garnered critical acclaim for both performances. The two films earned Locsin the Star Award for Movies for Movie Actress of the Year and the Box-Office Entertainment Award for Film Actress of the Year, winning both awards two consecutive years. In 2013, she won the FAMAS Award and the Film Academy of the Philippines Award for best actress for her performance in One More Try.

While she became famous for her work in the entertainment industry, Angel Locsin has always been noted for her philanthropic work. With the heart to serve others, she used her influence and platform to continuously help and change the lives of Filipinos whenever she can. She was also included in Forbes “Heroes of Philanthropy 2019” in Asia for her charitable works. Not only does she donate financial aid to those affected, Angel Locsin personally goes to help distribute goods to people in need whenever she can. Just recently, she also organized a community pantry in celebration of her birthday, with the goal to provide relief goods to people who are affected by the community quarantine implementations in NCR.
